Frequently Asked Questions
So why does Voter Power matter?
Are you interested in participating in the democratic process? Voter power can tell you where your vote, your campaign volunteering, and your political donations will have the most impact. It highlights the critical elections that can swing the balance of legislatures.

What elections should I look for?
Our dropdown includes candidate and voter power information for the Presidential race, Senate races, House of Representatives' races, Gubernatorial races, State Legislature's races, and Ballot measures. Different races have different voter powers, and for a given area, our website defines these individually. All of this information can be observed by using the dropdown on a relevant state or by searching a local address. Note that more elections will appear on your ballot apart from these races. Our database is not an exhaustive list of every election race in one area.

What is in these different ballot initiative categories?
Democracy Repair initiatives are ballot measures that impact the way voters interact with government. They could be about implementing electoral systems like ranked choice, voter ID regulations, adjusting which positions in government are appointed versus elected, or many other elements of the democratic process.
Direct Democracy initiatives are ballot measures that serve as a direct policy implementation directives to the government. These are typically things you would expect a legislature to do- adjust taxes, change the minimum wage, adjust zoning regulations, etc. Due to inaction or frustration, people are trying to implement them through a ballot measure instead.
Civil Liberties initiatives are direct democracy ballot measures specifically concerning policies surrounding civil liberties. Drug classifications, LGBTQ rights, and similar issues fall under this category.
